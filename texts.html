Primary Sources

The Textual Foundation

Our primary sources span three traditions: Vedantic (philosophy and path), Agamic (devotion and ritual), and Nyaya-Vaisheshika (epistemology and natural philosophy). Each tradition operates at its own level and serves its own purpose. Together they cover the full range of what a sincere practitioner needs: understanding of what is ultimately real, tools for engaging the empirical world honestly, and a ritual form that keeps the heart alive through the long practice.

The Garjanaa Document

Complete Reference Text

The Garjanaa Document is this community's complete internal guide: the full Advaita Vedanta philosophy (35 chapters), the Garjanaa Smriti (four books), the four upayas, the Sadhan and Jnana circles, rites of passage, and all appendices, organized into four Khandas in one reference text.

Vidya Khanda

Soteriology, axiology, Aanvikshiki epistemology, full Advaita ontology, Pancha-devata, and the four upayas.

Yoga Khanda

The four yogas, Raja Yoga in full, qualifications, chitta-shuddhi, naishkarmya-siddhi, Manonasha, Vasanakshaya, Tattvajnana, self-inquiry, jivanmukti.

Kriya Khanda

Daily practice framework, seva, Sadhan and Jnana circles, all ten practices in detail.

Charya Khanda

Rituals, rites of passage, monthly meeting, community principles, conflict resolution, safeguards, questions seekers ask, reading path, and glossary.

Vedantic Texts

The Philosophy and Path

Sarvavedantasiddhantasara Sangraha
Compendium of All Vedantic Conclusions

A comprehensive digest of all Vedantic conclusions by Sri Madhusudana Sarasvati. This masterwork systematically presents the essence of the entire Vedantic tradition — from the Upanishadic declarations to the Brahma Sutras to the Bhagavad Gita — demonstrating how all paths converge in the non-dual recognition of Brahman as one's own Self.

Key teaching: The Self is of the nature of pure Consciousness, eternal, infinite, and free. Ignorance (avidya) conceals this truth and projects the appearance of bondage. Through the grace of the guru — which is the teaching itself — this ignorance is dispelled, and the ever-free Self shines forth.

VedantaSystematicPrakarana
Tattvabodha
Knowledge of Truth

Attributed to Adi Shankaracharya, this is the gateway text for beginners in Advaita Vedanta. In a systematic dialogue between guru and disciple, it introduces every technical term required for self-inquiry: Sadhana Chatushtaya (fourfold qualifications), Sharira Traya (three bodies), Avastha Traya (three states), Pancha Kosha (five sheaths), and the great Mahavakya Tat Tvam Asi.

Key teaching: The Self (Atman) is not the body, mind, or intellect — it is identical with Brahman, the infinite. Liberation (moksha) is not something to be attained but recognized: the removal of ignorance reveals what was always true.

PrakaranaBeginnerShankara
Upadesha Sahasri
A Thousand Teachings

Adi Shankaracharya's most detailed pedagogical work on Advaita Vedanta. Composed in both prose and verse, it unfolds across eighteen chapters covering the entire path: from the necessity of the guru (the teaching), through the analysis of the Self, to the direct recognition of Brahman. It is the roar of the guru's voice, preserved in text.

Key teaching: The guru's teaching (upadesha) is the direct means to liberation. The Self is not an object to be known but the very subject, the witness of all knowing. When the disciple hears, reflects, and meditates upon this teaching with the fourfold qualifications, ignorance is destroyed and the Self stands revealed as it always was.

ShankaraPedagogicalComprehensive
Bhagavad Gita
The Song of the Lord

The ethical and psychological backbone of the entire path. Contains the portraits of the Sthitaprajna (person of steady wisdom) and the mature devotee, the complete teaching of Karma Yoga, the Daivi and Asuri Sampada, and Krishna's direct declaration that all of this is Brahman. Begin here if you are new to this tradition.

CoreEthicsKarma Yoga
Yoga Vasishtha
The Teaching of Vasishtha to Rama

A vast dialogue on the nature of consciousness, liberation, and the seven stages of awakening, taught by the sage Vasishtha to Rama. Particularly valued for its psychological precision and its account of the Jivanmukta, one liberated while fully alive. The four gatekeepers of liberation it names — tranquility, contentment, self-inquiry, and the company of the wise — form a practical checklist. The triple path of Manonasha, Vasanakshaya, and Tattvajnana is drawn from this text.

Tripura Rahasya
The Secret of Tripura

An Advaita teaching in a devotional frame centered on the Divine Mother. Demonstrates how sincere devotion, followed to its natural depth, converges completely with direct self-knowledge. Valuable for those drawn to the integration of bhakti and jnana.

BhaktiJnanaShakta
The Upanishads
The Philosophical Source of Advaita

The Mandukya (the three states and Turiya), Brihadaranyaka (the Mahavakyas and the nature of Brahman), Chandogya (Tat Tvam Asi), and Kena (the nature of pure awareness) are central. The Advaita guide works through all of these in careful detail with Shankara's commentary.

SourceUpanishadicFundamental
Ramayana and Mahabharata
Dharma Lived as a Life

The Ramayana offers Rama's sixteen qualities as a precise portrait of realized character in the world. The Mahabharata contains the Vidura Niti, the Sanatsujatiya, and the Bhagavad Gita itself, along with countless teachings on right conduct where simple answers are never enough.

ItihasaDharmaConduct
Srimad Bhagavatam and Devi Bhagavatam
The Devotional Heart

The Bhagavatam's saints demonstrate that sincere practice shows up in daily conduct, not private experience. The Devi Bhagavatam contains the Goddess's own non-dual teaching, affirming that the Shakta path arrives at the same recognition as the jnana path.

PuranaDevotionalConduct
Ashtavakra Gita
Song of Ashtavakra

The most uncompromising text in the tradition, pointing at liberation immediately. Its opening verse distills the entire path: cultivate forgiveness, straightforwardness, compassion, contentment, and truthfulness as nectar. These five dissolve the ego's grip precisely because each removes a specific mechanism the ego runs on.

RadicalDirectDialogue
Vivekachudamani
Crest Jewel of Discrimination

580 verses on the direct path to liberation. It begins with the rarity of human birth and the urgency of spiritual practice, moves through the discrimination between the real and the unreal, and culminates in the direct experience of Brahman as one's own Self.

ShankaraDevotionalPractical
Avadhuta Gita
Song of the Free Soul

The song of the free soul — Dattatreya's own teaching on the absolute nature of reality. It declares with thunderous certainty: "I am not the body, nor the mind, nor the intellect. I am pure Consciousness, free from all attributes. There is no bondage, no liberation, no seeker, no sought."

Agamic Texts

Devotion and Ritual

The Agamas
Scriptural Foundation of Temple Worship

The scriptural foundation of temple worship, ritual, mantra, and the Pancha-devata practice. The Shaiva, Vaishnava, and Shakta Agamas ground devotional practice in a living, tested tradition. Our simplified daily puja draws on the principles of Panchopachara (five-fold offering) found throughout the Agamic literature, adapted for practice without a human guru or formal initiation.

AgamaRitualMantra
Mahanirvana Tantra
The Great Liberation Tantra

A Shakta text that explicitly adapts ancient rites to modern conditions, affirming that sincere practice conducted with understanding rather than merely external form is available to all regardless of birth or prior initiation. The Sadhan Chakra and Jnana Chakra described in this text are the inspiration for the Garjanaa circles. One of the clearest scriptural authorities for the community's approach to simplified Agamic practice.

Nyaya-Vaisheshika and Aanvikshiki

Epistemology and Natural Philosophy

Nyaya Sutras (Gautama)
The Logic of Valid Knowledge

The foundational text of the Nyaya school, systematizing the theory of valid knowledge (pramana), logical reasoning (anumana), the structure of valid argument, and the catalog of logical fallacies (hetvabhasa). The epistemological framework this community applies to all empirical claims. The four pramanas — perception, inference, analogy, and reliable testimony — are the basis for critical thinking in any domain.

NyayaLogicEpistemology
Vaisheshika Sutras (Kanada)
The Categories of Reality

The foundational text of the Vaisheshika school, providing a systematic categorization of reality through the seven padarthas: substance, quality, motion, universal, particular, inherence, and non-existence. This framework applies at the vyavaharika (empirical) level and is compatible with modern scientific categorization of natural phenomena.

VaisheshikaCategoriesNatural Philosophy
Arthashastra (Kautilya) on Aanvikshiki
The Lamp of All Sciences

Describes Aanvikshiki as "the lamp of all sciences, the resource for all actions, and the support of all dharmas." The principle it establishes — that all knowledge claims in the empirical domain are subject to disciplined critical examination — is the ancestor of the scientific method and the governing standard for this community's engagement with empirical questions.

Further Texts

Additional Resources

Yoga Sutras of Patanjali
The Systematic Map of Raja Yoga

The systematic map of Raja Yoga: the ethical foundation of Yamas and Niyamas, followed by posture, breath regulation, sense withdrawal, concentration, and meditative absorption. Patanjali's two-word definition of posture, sthira-sukham (steady and comfortable), applies equally to the whole of practice. The full treatment of Patanjali's system is in the Yoga Khanda of the complete document.

YogaSystematicPractice
Vijnanabhairava Tantra
112 Doors to Recognition

A Kashmir Shaiva text presenting 112 dharanas (recognition practices) through which one's nature as pure consciousness can be recognized in the midst of ordinary activities. Central to the Shambhava-upaya described in the Yoga Khanda. Many of the dharanas require no formal sitting context and are fully compatible with daily life.

Kashmir ShaivaDharanaDaily Life
Drig-Drsya-Viveka
Discrimination Between Seer and Seen

A concise analytical text on the discrimination between the Seer (Drig) and the Seen (Drsya). Through systematic inquiry, it demonstrates that the Self is the pure witness, untouched by the modifications of the mind and the objects of perception.

AnalyticalWitnessConcise
Aparokshanubhuti
Direct Experience

A practical manual of 144 verses on the direct means to Self-realization. It covers the qualifications, the method of inquiry, the nature of the Self, and the signs of the jivanmukta (liberated while living).
