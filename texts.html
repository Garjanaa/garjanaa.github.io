Primary Sources

The Scriptures We Draw From

We transmit what named texts actually say. Our twelve primary sources are the Bhagavad Gītā, Yoga Vāsiṣṭha, Tripurā Rahasya, the Upaniṣads, Rāmāyaṇa, Mahābhārata, Śrīmad Bhāgavatam, Devī Bhāgavatam, Aṣṭāvakra Gītā, the Āgamas, the Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali, and the Mahānirvāṇa Tantra. Each is described below with guidance on where to begin.

Bhagavad Gītā

The ethical and psychological backbone, containing the portraits of the Sthitaprajña and Bhakta.

Yoga Vāsiṣṭha

A vast dialogue on the nature of consciousness, liberation, and the seven stages.

Upaniṣads

The philosophical wellspring of Advaita — Māṇḍūkya, Bṛhadāraṇyaka, Chāndogya, and Kena are central.

Rāmāyaṇa & Mahābhārata

Dharma lived as a life, and dharma in all its complexity — containing the Vidura Nīti, Sanatsujātīya, and the Bhagavad Gītā.

Śrīmad Bhāgavatam & Devī Bhāgavatam

The devotional heart, universal ethics, and the Goddess’s own non‑dual teaching.
