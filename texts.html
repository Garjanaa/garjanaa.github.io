Primary Sources

The Scriptures We Draw From

We transmit what named texts actually say. Our twelve primary sources are the Bhagavad Gītā, Yoga Vāsiṣṭha, Tripūrā Rahasya, the Upaniṣads, Rāmāyaṇa, Mahābhārata, Śrīmad Bhāgavatam, Devī Bhāgavatam, Aṣṭāvakra Gītā, the Āgamas, the Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali, and the Mahānirvāṇa Tantra. Each is described below with guidance on where to begin.

The Garjanaa Document

The Garjanaa Document is this community’s own complete guide to the path: the philosophy, the practices, and the codes of conduct that govern how we live and work together. It comprises two companion volumes, both available in full below.

Volume I
Advaita Vedanta: A Clear and Practical Guide
A complete 35‑chapter guide to the philosophy and practice of non‑duality, drawing on Shankara, Ramana Maharshi, Ramsukhdas ji, and the primary source texts. Covers the Three Bodies, Five Sheaths, Three States of Experience, Brahman, Atman, Maya, the Mahavakyas, the path of self‑inquiry, and liberation while living. Written to be read without prior knowledge and practiced without a guru.
Read the Advaita Guide
Volume II
Codes of Conduct: The Garjanaa Smriti
A complete manual for living the path in the twenty‑first century. Four books: Vidyā Khaṇḍa (knowledge and the five streams), Yoga Khaṇḍa (union of jnāna and bhakti), Kriyā Khaṇḍa (the ten daily practices in full detail), and Ācāra Khaṇḍa (community conduct, Nīti, the ten rites of passage, conflict resolution, and safeguards against corruption). Non‑sectarian, non‑hereditary, casteless, open to all who come sincerely.

The Twelve Primary Sources

Bhagavad Gītā

The ethical and psychological backbone of the entire path, containing the portraits of the Sthitaprajña (person of steady wisdom) and the Bhakta (mature devotee), the teaching of Karma Yoga, the Daivī and Āsurī Sampadā, and Krishna’s direct declaration that Vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti: Vasudeva is everything. Begin here if you are new to this tradition.

Yoga Vāsiṣṭha

A vast dialogue on the nature of consciousness, liberation, and the seven stages of awakening, taught by the sage Vasiṣṭha to Prince Rama. Particularly valued for its psychological precision, its account of the Jīvanmukta (one liberated while alive), and the four gatekeepers of liberation: tranquility, contentment, self‑inquiry, and the company of the wise.

Tripūrā Rahasya

An Advaita teaching in a devotional frame centered on the Divine Mother, valuable for those drawn to the integration of jñāna and bhakti. Shows how devotion, sincerely followed to its natural depth, converges completely with direct self‑knowledge.

Upaniṣads

The philosophical wellspring of Advaita. The Māṇḍūkya (the three states and Turiya), Bṛhadāraṇyaka (the Mahavakyas and the nature of Brahman), Chāndogya (Tat Tvam Asi), and Kena (the nature of pure awareness) are central. The Advaita Guide in Volume I of the Garjanaa Document works through all of these in careful detail.

Rāmāyaṇa & Mahābhārata

Dharma lived as a life, and dharma in all its complexity. The Rāmāyaṇa offers Rama’s sixteen qualities as a precise portrait of realized character. The Mahābhārata contains the Vidura Nīti, the Sanatsujātīya, and the Bhagavad Gītā itself, along with countless teachings on right conduct in a world where simple answers are never enough.

Śrīmad Bhāgavatam & Devī Bhāgavatam

The devotional heart of the tradition. The Bhāgavatam’s saints, Prahlāda, Dhruva, Kapila, demonstrate that sincere practice shows up in daily conduct, not private experience. The Devī Bhāgavatam contains the Goddess’s own non‑dual teaching, affirming that the Śākta path arrives at the same recognition as the jñāna path.

Aṣṭāvakra Gītā

The most uncompromising text in the tradition, pointing at liberation immediately rather than building toward it gradually. Its opening verse distills the entire path: cultivate forgiveness, straightforwardness, compassion, contentment, and truthfulness as nectar. These five dissolve the ego’s grip precisely because each removes a specific mechanism the ego runs on.

The Āgamas

The scriptural foundation of temple worship, ritual, mantra, and the five streams of Pañcadevatā practice described in the Garjanaa Codes of Conduct. The Śaiva, Vaiṣṇava, and Śākta Āgamas ground devotional practice in a living, tested tradition stretching back millennia.

Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali

The systematic map of Rāja Yoga: the ethical foundation of Yamas and Niyamas, followed by posture, breath regulation, sense withdrawal, concentration, and meditative absorption. Patañjali’s two‑word definition of posture, sthira‑sukham (steady and comfortable), applies equally to the whole of practice: steadiness and ease, not effort and strain.

Mahānirvāṇa Tantra

A Śākta text that adapts the ancient rites to the conditions of the Kaliyuga, affirming that sincere practice, conducted with understanding rather than merely external form, is available to all regardless of birth or prior initiation. Foundational to the community’s rite‑of‑passage structure described in the Codes of Conduct.

A reading path through these texts, from most accessible to most demanding, is given in full in Chapter 34 of the Advaita Guide.
