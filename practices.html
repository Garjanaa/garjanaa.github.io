The Living Path

Inner Toolkit

Our practices are drawn directly from the Yoga Vāsiṣṭha, Tripurā Rahasya, and Mahānirvāṇa Tantra. They are designed to settle the body, quiet the mind, and turn attention toward its source.

Self‑Inquiry (Vicāra)

Ask “Who am I?” — not as a mantra, but as a living question. Trace the sense of “I” back to its source in pure awareness. This is the central practice of Advaita.

The Witness Attitude

Throughout the day, recognize yourself as the unattached witness of thoughts, emotions, and events. The witness is always steady, never shaken.

Mantra & Japa

The community mantra — “Kaalee‑Durge Radheshyaam, Gaurishankar Seetaaraam” — is repeated with a mālā, settling the mind into a single sacred current.

Kīrtan & Nyāsa

Devotional singing and the ritual consecration of the body as a temple bring the sacred into the senses and the community into one voice.

Study & Service

Regular engagement with the texts (Svādhyāya) and selfless service (Sevā) ground the practice in everyday life.

The Seven Stages of Awakening

From the Yoga Vāsiṣṭha, the seven bhūmikās describe the natural unfolding of the mind: good desire, self‑inquiry, thinning of the mind, attainment of equanimity, non‑attachment, seeing through objects, and the final state of Turīya — the ever‑present witness.
