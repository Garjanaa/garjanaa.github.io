Advaita Vedanta

The Philosophy of Non‑Duality

Our path rests on the understanding that consciousness is not divided — that the individual self (jīva) and the universal ground (Brahman) are not two. This is the core of Advaita Vedānta.

The Mahāvākyas

The Upaniṣads distill this truth into four great sayings: Ahaṁ Brahmāsmi (I am Brahman), Tat Tvam Asi (That thou art), Prajñānaṁ Brahma (Consciousness is Brahman), and Ayam Ātmā Brahma (This self is Brahman).

Three Orders of Reality

Advaita distinguishes the absolute (Paramārthika), the conventional (Vyāvahārika), and the apparent (Prātibhāsika) — helping us navigate the world while realizing the non‑dual truth.

Four Paths to Liberation

We integrate Jñāna (knowledge), Bhakti (devotion), Karma (selfless action), and Rāja (meditation) — not as competitors but as convergent streams.

The Values We Cultivate

The Daivī Sampada (divine qualities) from the Bhagavad Gītā — fearlessness, purity, steadiness, compassion, and more — are our practical horizon. We also recognize the Asurī Sampada (demonic qualities) as the shadow of the ego, to be seen clearly and dissolved through inquiry.

These values are described with clinical precision in our source texts, not as abstract ideals but as living targets for practice.
